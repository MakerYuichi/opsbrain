7.4.1 Purpose of Argon Purging
Argon purging (also termed argon stirring or bottom blowing) serves three
essential metallurgical functions:
  (a) Temperature homogenisation: eliminates stratified hot and cold zones
      within the melt, reducing localised thermal stress on the ladle refractory.
  (b) Chemical homogenisation: ensures uniform alloy and inclusion distribution;
      promotes desulphurisation and removal of dissolved hydrogen.
  (c) Gas pocket elimination: displaces trapped hydrogen, nitrogen, and residual
      argon pockets from the liquid steel bath. Failure to eliminate such pockets
      creates risk of sudden volumetric gas expansion if mechanical agitation
      (e.g., ladle rotation, tilt, or slide gate opening) disturbs the pocket
      at a later stage.

CRITICAL WARNING:
If a ladle furnace heat is processed without argon purging, the ladle MUST
NOT be transferred to casting without a mandatory additional holding period
of minimum 15 minutes at temperature under slag cover, followed by a manual
confirmation from the furnace coordinator that surface activity (boiling/
turbulence) is absent. This check MUST be recorded.

Failure to observe this precaution risks a Type-3 explosion event: rapid
gas pocket expansion during ladle rotation or slide-gate activation at the
casting platform, with risk of ladle tipover and catastrophic hot metal spill.

7.4.2 Minimum Purging Parameters
  — Duration: 15–25 minutes (grade-dependent; refer Appendix B)
  — Flow rate: 20–35 NL/min (set via FCV-APS controller)
  — Lance insertion depth: 800mm ± 50mm from ladle base

7.4.3 Equipment Defect Escalation
If the argon purge system (lance, FCV controller, or gas supply) is found
defective during a heat sequence, the defect MUST be escalated to the SMS
Superintendent immediately. Heat processing without purging is permitted
ONLY with written Superintendent sign-off and only for a single heat.
Recurring deferrals are NOT permitted without a formal risk assessment.

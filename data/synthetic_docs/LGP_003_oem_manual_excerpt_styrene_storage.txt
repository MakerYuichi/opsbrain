4.7.1 Pre-Shutdown Inhibitor Dosing
Prior to any plant shutdown exceeding 7 days, the operator MUST perform an
inhibitor top-up to achieve a minimum TBC concentration of 15 ppm in the
liquid phase. For shutdowns exceeding 30 days, the minimum concentration
increases to 25 ppm. Dissolved oxygen must be verified ≥5 ppm via grab sample
before closing tank inspection ports.

WARNING: TBC inhibitor is effective only in the presence of dissolved oxygen.
Stagnant tanks with no circulation will develop an oxygen gradient; liquid near
the tank bottom may become oxygen-depleted within 7–14 days depending on
temperature. Do not assume that a dosed tank is protected indefinitely.

4.7.2 Cooling System Requirements During Shutdown
The brine chiller circuit MUST remain in operation during any shutdown where
ambient temperatures are forecast to exceed 25°C. Acceptable tank temperature
is ≤20°C. If chiller is taken offline for maintenance, maximum allowable
duration is 72 hours; above this, styrene must be transferred to an alternate
tank with active cooling.

CAUTION: Auto-polymerisation of styrene is an exothermic reaction. Once
initiated above ~30°C, the reaction is self-accelerating. At temperatures
above 52°C, TBC inhibitor loses effectiveness. The heat of polymerisation
(approximately 660 kJ/kg) will further raise tank temperature in a runaway
feedback loop. Emergency venting alone will NOT arrest this reaction.

4.7.3 Temperature Monitoring
At least one calibrated RTD (resistance temperature detector) probe must be
installed at mid-level on each tank. Alarms shall be configured:
  — Warning alarm: 18°C
  — Critical alarm: 25°C
  — Emergency shutdown trigger: 30°C

If the alarm system is offline for any reason, manual temperature readings must
be recorded every 4 hours and initialled by a qualified operator.

4.7.4 Inspection Before Restart
Before re-commissioning following a shutdown exceeding 14 days:
(a) Sample liquid styrene from top and bottom of tank for TBC concentration
    and inhibitor effectiveness testing.
(b) Check dissolved oxygen at three depths.
(c) Verify chiller brine flow rate before starting transfer pumps.
(d) Do not initiate transfers until temperature ≤18°C is confirmed.

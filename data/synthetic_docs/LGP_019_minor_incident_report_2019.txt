DOCUMENT ID: LGP-INCIDENT-019
TYPE: Minor Incident Report
FACILITY: Horizon Chemicals Pvt. Ltd., Eastport Industrial Zone
DATE: 2019-07-14
REPORTED BY: Shift Engineer D. Prasad
INCIDENT CATEGORY: Near-Miss / Instrumentation Failure
SEVERITY: Low (no injury, no release)

--- DESCRIPTION ---

At 14:45, during routine tank farm walkthrough, Shift Engineer D. Prasad
noticed that the temperature display for Tank ST-11 on the main control panel
was reading 27.3°C — above the acceptable limit of 20°C.

This reading was later determined to be a TRUE reading (not a sensor fault)
corresponding to a chiller system partial flow restriction (brine strainer
partially blocked). Once the strainer was cleaned and chiller flow restored,
ST-11 temperature returned to 18.2°C within 4 hours.

HOWEVER: The temperature alarm (setpoint 18°C warning, 25°C critical) had NOT
triggered despite the 27.3°C reading. Investigation found the alarm relay
contacts corroded. Temperature had apparently been above 20°C for an estimated
8–12 hours prior to being noticed visually by the engineer.

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS TAKEN:
1. Brine strainer cleaned. [DONE, 2019-07-14]
2. Alarm relay replaced. [DONE, 2019-07-16]
3. Recommended: add second independent high-temp alarm on ST-11. [NOT DONE —
   deferred per site engineer response: "one alarm should be sufficient."]
4. Recommended: monthly alarm function test for all tank farm instruments.
   [NOT DONE — no procedure created, no schedule set.]

RISK REGISTER ENTRY: Not made (incident classified as low-severity near-miss;
process engineer decided risk register entry not warranted).

ROOT CAUSE STATEMENT: "Partial chiller blockage caused transient temperature
exceedance. Corrected. No recurrence expected."

REMARKS: No cross-reference made to Finding 1.2 in the 2019 DISH audit
(FT-11 flow sensor anomaly on same tank) — these two separate anomaly records
on the same critical asset were never connected.
